Lets get into the course:

Maker100 Leaders Robotics

pre-engineers can be leaders but leaders do not need to be pre-engineers. Also pre-engineers don't have to become engineers they have skills that 
work in other fields, eiher way this course will challenge everyone taking it. Record all +60 assignments on a public or private chart, solved in any oder,
Rule of Lead Three: if you are the first to solve an issue you must teach three others, and they are responsible to help 3 more students, the total class highest 
mark 90%-100% comes from the class tone
of people helping each other. Onces the 40-60 assignments are comlete they do their level 1 and 2 final projects. Level one is a course pass, level 2 the higher mark. 
Multiple people are expected to get the highest mark, I am fine with 10 students all getting the top grade. It hurts no one
and massively improves those pre-engineers communication skills and the tone of the class.

The class always starts with the Analog fire wall: code is summarized on paper, before loading onto a microcontroller for testing before any wires are attached, 
circuit diagrams are drawn by hand and checked by a lead three before wired and again power is connected. Students are encouraged to plan before they get to class.  

Leaders do the assignments and extend them, make it their own, dig deeper about it, think about uses for it. That is the most powerful part of the course, balancing
the shear volume of work with the massive aplications of almost every assignment. By the way the course is at  https://github.com/hpssjellis/maker100-leaders-robotics 
is dynamic changing with the issues found, opensource, means free and it has links to the maker100 curriculum, dynamic pricelist, and webmcu-ai 

These students will learn arduino style basics, basic coding, sensors (light motion sound ...), actuators (motors LED's ...), ML(Machine Learning: Vision, Sound, Motion) 
and IoT (multi device communication). Some assignments are easy, some are challenging. The faster students solve the assignments the more time they have for final projects, 
which if finished they are expected to start another project.
