The Red Wire Story: Why AI Must Be DemocratizedIn my robotics class, a pre-engineer was having troubles over his level 2 final project: a robot arm with IoT sensors and a tiny vision‑ML loop. A classroom leader came over to help. The pre‑engineer was meticulous. And yet the system kept failing, working one moment, collapsing the next. Beside them stood a third student. Quiet. Barely passing, he spoke up gently:
“What if the red wire isn’t working?” The leader froze. The pre‑engineer sighed, but said, “Fine. Let’s swap the VCC, with a different red wire for 3V3. They did. And instantly, the entire robot came alive. Smooth. Stable. Perfect.The breakthrough didn’t come from expertise. It came from inclusion. From giving space to the voice no one expected to matter. Now imagine a world where only a handful of powerful cloud LLM companies and their employees get to decide how we solve society’s hardest problems. That’s a world with only two students at the table. We need the third student.
We need the unexpected insight.
We need the person who sees the simple truth the experts miss. Democratizing AI isn’t optional, it’s how we keep the future from failing because of a bad VCC line. And for the record:
In my robotics class, we never throw away equipment students call “broken.”
But that day, we held a little ceremony, and tossed that red VCC wire straight into the garbage.
