🎤 Maker100 Leaders Robotics: Presentation Script
Section 1: The Shock & The Question (The Hook)
Slide 1: Title Slide
"Good morning/afternoon. I’m Jeremy Ellis. And today, I’m not here to talk about another robotics club. I’m here to talk about the future of leadership. I’m here to talk about Maker100 Leaders Robotics."

Slide 2: The Provocative Question
"Let me start with a question that I believe every leader, every educator, and every student needs to answer: Should leaders of the future have a grassroots understanding of Artificial Intelligence?"

Slide 3: The Reality Check
"AI isn't science fiction anymore; it’s reshaping policy, jobs, and daily life. But here is the critical gap: Most people are just using AI; they are not building anything physical with it. Ignorance in this space is a massive societal vulnerability. Our current education system is failing to equip students with this foundational literacy."

Slide 4: The Thesis Statement
"My mission is simple: We are moving beyond consumption. We are moving toward creation. We are moving from being AI consumers to becoming AI architects. That is what Maker100 Leaders Robotics is about."

Section 2: The Credibility & The Vision
Slide 5: Who Am I? (Briefly)
"Why listen to me? I’m a Canadian high school technology teacher who looked at neural networks in the 90s and realized I needed to understand them to teach them. For the last decade, I’ve focused on the intersection of two colliding fields: WebAI and TinyML."

Slide 6: The AI Teaching Assistant
"This brings me to the heart of our approach: Generative AI tools are not cheating mechanisms; they are the most powerful, accessible teaching assistants ever created. They allow students to ask the right questions, evaluate the answers, and learn how to code through rapid iteration."

Slide 7: The Analog Firewall
"But how do we ensure actual learning happens? We implement an Analog Firewall. Students can use AI to generate code, but to pass, they must build the physical circuit diagram from memory and talk me through the code line-by-line. The proof of understanding is entirely human. If you can't explain it, you didn't build it."

Section 3: The Innovation Showcase (The Demos)
Slide 8: Innovation 1: Sovereign AI (WebLLM PWA)
"The biggest barrier to learning AI is cloud access and subscription costs. We smash that barrier using a Progressive Web App that brings a powerful LLM directly into the browser. Watch this." (Transition to Demo 1)

Slide 9: DEMO 1: WebLLM Offline
(During Demo 1: Show the PWA running completely offline.) "No internet. No cloud fees. The intelligence stays entirely on the machine. This is digital autonomy for the classroom."

Slide 10: Innovation 2: On-Device Edge AI
"What happens when we take that intelligence and shrink it down to physical hardware? We use TinyML to embed machine learning directly onto microcontrollers." (Transition to Demo 2)

Slide 11: DEMO 2: WebMCU-AI in Action
(During Demo 2: Show a fingertip-sized XIAO board running a vision model.) "This is Edge AI. Zero cloud dependency. High-speed, private, local intelligence running on a tiny chip."

Slide 12: The Full Browser-to-Hardware Pipeline
"This isn't just two separate tools. This is a complete, open-source pipeline. From gathering raw sensor data in a browser, to training the model locally, to deploying it onto a physical device via WebSerial. Students own the entire stack. No black boxes. No vendor lock-in."

Section 4: The Classroom Reality
Slide 13: The Hardware & The Structure
"We use the ultra-affordable XIAO ESP32-S3 ML Kit—packed with a camera, microphone, and motion sensors. To scale this in a classroom, we use a social protocol called 'Lead Three': the first student to solve a complex engineering challenge must immediately teach three peers. Knowledge is decentralized, not hoarded."

Slide 14: The Student Spectrum
"We cater to three student types: Users who gain big-picture AI leadership; Makers who execute and build physical prototypes; and Engineers who love the friction of first principles. Every student has a pathway to dig deeper."

Section 5: The Call to Action (The Ask)
Slide 15: The Global Pilot & The Ask
"This is a proven, battle-tested curriculum. For the upcoming academic year, we are launching a global pilot with schools in the Global South to prove it scales in low-resource environments. The hardware cost is just $200 per student. We are looking for visionary partners to fund a classroom cohort or step up as one of our pilot schools."

Slide 16: Final Vision
"We are not just building robots. We are building a sovereign generation of leaders who can build, audit, and direct the AI revolution. Let’s stop waiting for the future. Let’s start building it. Come find me afterward, let's get to work, and let's meet next year with the data. Thank you."
